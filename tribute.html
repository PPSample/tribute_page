  • Nissankara Savitri was an Indian actress, singer, and filmmaker known for her works primarily in Telugu and Tamil films.
  • She was born on 6th December 1934
  • Her parents were Subhadramma and Guravayya. Her father died when she was six months old, after which her mother took Savitri and an older sibling, Maruti to live with an aunt and uncle.
  • She was one of the highest-paid and most popular Indian actresses in the 1950s and '60s.
  • She is popularly known by the epithets Mahanati (transl. The great actress) in Telugu and Nadigaiyar Thilagam (transl. Doyen of all the actresses) in Tamil.
  • In a career spanning three decades, Savitri starred in more than 250 films. Her first significant role was in the 1952 film Pelli Chesi Choodu.
  • Savitri was also one of the top Tamil actresses of her era. She acted with major stalwarts, such as M.G.R, Sivaji Ganesan and her husband, Gemini Ganesan, mostly with the latter. Her notable Tamil works include Kalathur Kannamma (1959), Pasamalar (1961), Pava Mannippu (1961), Paarthal Pasi Theerum (1962), Karpagam (1963), Karnan (1963), Kai Koduttha Dheivam, Navarathri (1964), and Thiruvilaiyadal (1965).
  • Inspired from her life , A film was made named "Mahanati" .
  • She was very active in social activities and run many trusts.
  • Savitri died on 26 December 1981, at the age of 47, after being in a coma for 19 months. She had developed diabetes and high blood pressure.
